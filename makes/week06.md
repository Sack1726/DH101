# How Can We Analyze Texts? Softwares? AI?
Text Analyzed: All 33 hymns within the Homeric Hymns collection, translated by Christopher
Kelk
## On the Homeric Hymns (if you didn’t know): The Homeric Hymns are a collection of ancient
Greek hymns composed by various poets and later additions. The hymns were brought together
into a collection in the 1st century BC, but the hymns themselves date between 700-300 BC.
They are called “Homeric” because the texts are written in the style of the Greek poet Homer.

This prevalence supports the description of
these mythological stories as being divine
myths which are myths that explain why the

Voyant’s tools allowed me to easily see parallels between different hymns and how they
classify related subjects (especially epithets), but I could not as easily see why they mattered.
There can be grand descriptions of a figure or setting that might seem important to a narrative,
but in reality is not entirely necessary in understanding the text.

## 🤖 GPT Analysis (Summary)
Prompt:
“Provide a literary analysis of the Homeric Hymns, focusing on symbols, themes, imagery, and
any other literary elements you find important.”
GPT explained a general overview of the hymns, identifying themes such as “Order vs.
Chaos” and “Divine Power and Presence.” Surprisingly, it identified specific symbols like grain
relating to Demeter and the lyre relating to Apollo, however, the actual descriptions themselves
did not frequently explain beyond stating. As a matter of fact, no exact quotations were given in
the response to reference any literary element.
Another point I noticed was that it heavily focused on the larger hymns, rarely
mentioning any of the shorter ones. I also expected more historical context of the Ancient world
to be applied to the literary analysis, but this was often glossed over. At times, it felt more like I
was reading an analysis by a middle school teacher who disregarded history.

## 🔁 Comparison
Voyant’s tools allowed for me to see more concrete textual patterns. For example, some
of these hymns are actually combinations of different texts put together, sometimes containing
new epithets which could be seen with Voyant. I could also see specific instances of wide
narrative changes such as the drop of the word Zeus and the increase of Apollo in relation to the
creation of his temple in Delphi. The only huge issue I found with Voyant is if I had never read
several of these hymns, then I would be completely lost in trying to find meaning in them. GPT
aided in providing insight into the hymns which corresponded to what was emphasized in
Voyant.
There are a couple problems with GPT’s analysis, however. No exact examples were
given to support any interpretations. Its analysis felt basic and limited, skipping what it deemed
as unimportant. With Voyant I could get the “why” but not the “what,” and with GPT I could
only get the “what” but not the “why.”


## Assignment Reflection
With using both Voyant and GPT, I began to see that their output mainly gave data rather
than any detailed analysis (especially Voyant). With that said, each had their own strength at a
certain analytical aspect and each had their own flaws.
For Voyant, I noticed some flaws after considering texts whose composure is more
complicated. For example, there are narratives from which the main subjects are never directly
referenced. Additionally, fundamental actions that are important to narratives are sometimes
never repeated to highlight them. Both of these would be missed by Voyant as only the frequency
of words is measured as data. This leads to another issue. While I can receive raw data
containing words, Voyant does not interpret what they mean or their significance.
To contrast, GPT can supplement Voyant’s issue of finding meaning in words, giving
interpretations of symbols, themes, and other literary elements. A deeper read of the output,
however, reveals its dryness. Oftentimes, claims are given without any evidence, and sometimes
when evidence is given or when asked to, it can hallucinate.
Both Voyant and GPT’s outputs are fast and give data, but are limited when it comes to
an actual analysis of a text with a specific unique understanding. This Make has made me realize
the viewpoint that everything is built on tradition or rather that tradition provides a foundation to
build on. While both Voyant and GPT can provide inspiration, neither of them can be sure of an
exact interpretation in a literary text. People themselves have the ability to shape meaning
because of our personal experiences and cultural backgrounds.

## Assignment Reflection
What changes when an image repeats instead of standing still?
●	There is a limit even if immense to what a static image can convey. While every subject could be in sight, a repeating image differs in that it can present or force a certain idea at a select time. This can be fast for some which forces the viewer to rewatch the GIF loop to better understand what is trying to be conveyed.
How does looping affect attention, meaning, or emotion?
●	The looping reveals that people do not always pay attention to what they are viewing. I remember distinct times when I waited for a looping GIF to repeat, so that I could understand what was trying to be conveyed as a response to a topic.
Where do you see human choice in your GIF?
●	My recent interest in the Punic Wars led to creating a GIF relating to the subject. I quickly realized, however, the lack of visual material online, leading to a constraint. Every image I used relied on my personal choices to convey the origins of the Fabian strategy. The idea behind comparing the strategy to “The Tortoise and the Hare” fable was taken from a documentary I watched long ago and was specifically selected by me within these constraints.
Where do you see automation or pattern?
●	I only used AI to generate caption ideas for the GIF and edited one of the responses to use. The phrase “be like” is common for a wide range of memes online and fall under that pattern.
How does AI complicate ideas of authorship or originality?
●	A person does not necessarily have the ownership of the training data that the AI uses to generate an output. AI creations are composed of many materials, but instead of just being similar, they are exactly what these materials are, removing originality. There can be human authorship when selecting what to use, but at the same time it adds mud to the water of who truly has authorship in the end.

## Research Documentation
- What sources did you use?
● I used ten sources directly to research AI use cost and kilowatt-hour & carbon dioxide
usage. I focused on using academic sources (.edu) to get AI data. An article from the
Columbia Climate School led me to find a recent academic paper May 2025 by a research
group from the University of Rhode Island, the University of Tunis, and Providence
College about AI’s impact with energy and carbon emissions. For the rest of my research,
the sources are from nonprofit (.org) research or governmental (.gov) sources. There are a
couple sources from business I used when I synthesized watt usage and to check my
work. The formula I used to calculate carbon cost is kWh ✕ 0.37 = Total kg CO2e, which
I then converted all results to grams. The 0.37 is based on the U.S. net generation of 0.81
pounds of CO2 emissions per kWh. This number serves for an average intensity of
energy consumption.
- What data was hardest to find? & What uncertainties exist?
● The data that was hardest to find was practically every single bit of more accurate
information. A lot of articles regurgitate numbers for AI and its energy & carbon costs
but either don’t provide evidence or seem to use another source as evidence who then
itself does not provide any. While I know it seemed convincing to give up and use data I
am not too sure on, I kept researching for articles that gave more accurate (though not
perfect) information. A part that I noticed that was hard to find was how much Watt and
carbon is used per an AI prompt. There are a lot of dependent factors like length and
model, but it should not be that hard to give a rough estimate. I found that AI companies
themselves often significantly lowball their estimates if mentioned and are a fraction
compared to independent studies. This is not a hard action to understand as it would be
idiotic to invite any bad press when a person can simply remain silent on the issue. The
most blatant uncertainty is a true, not just a likely estimate, of the impacts of an AI
prompt. Even in the data I used for each AI, some standard deviations were quite high at
2.160, 3.663, and 1.779.


## Artist Statement
- What environmental cost most concerns you?
● What concerns me the most is the local impact that AI can have, most notably with data
centers. People can be typically disconnected from their environment impact, but your
own water system from your faucet being stressed reveals how directly a vital resource
humans use can be impacted by environmental issues.
- Can AI be sustainable? What would it take?
● While it can be easy to point out and say AI is not sustainable but in theory it can be and
that a checklist of scaling and efficiency must be accomplished, this kind of approach
rarely works. I would like to point out that since the 1990s, countries have publicly
promised to do actions at famous environment conventions to improve their impact yet
CO2 emissions and more are still worsening. Many promises of action are watered down,
never happen, or are postponed indefinitely. What it would take to make AI sustainable is
an entire societal attitude change. While you might think I would take a negative
likelihood of this occurring, the amount of drastic societal change within the past century
proves it may be easier than expected. With that said, I think the current path many
societies are heading toward today that is characterized by a new kind of irrationality will
prove extremely difficult to reverse or change for any specific concern. I’m not the first
person to write something like this but love, morality, beauty, and meaning aren't
scientifically measurable, yet we still consider them to be real, important, and rational.
- What is your responsibility?
● My personal responsibility for AI sustainability is the obvious obligation of efficient use
of AI such as prompt and token efficiently and knowing when to and not use AI. I think
an important responsibility is to not confuse “what can be measured” with “what is real.”
I can’t necessarily in an accurate manner measure, test, or see every part of AI’s impact,
but I know it is there from indirect signals.

## Attribution & AI Use
Originally, I tried to make a website like energy & carbon PDF infographic but realized a
website with calculations would give a way better picture, so I decided to salvage my
progress and use Visual Studio Code. Every single piece of text, layout between sections,
and exact color choice (Note: I did take the color code from each AI’s website to use) is
based on my personal design choice. I summarized the conversation and what was
designed below:
1. User sees multiple buttons with each AI to select.
2. User can click on the buttons which ask which model of it if there is an option for it (Only Gemini doesn't have it).
3. A text box appears that asks for a prompt to be entered.
4. The user's entered prompt is scanned for its character amount.
5. This character amount from the user's prompt is used to calculate the energy (Watt-hours) and carbon (gCO2e) costs.
6. The costs are displayed to the user after I check measurements.
7. Display a return button for the user to repeat.

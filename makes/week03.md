## ✍️ Assignment Reflection
	“What does AI think a ‘normal’ person looks like?”
When I started to develop a prompt to input into DALL-E, I quickly found out that overcomplicating subjects like giving my specific ethnic background would overwhelm the AI. When this happened, the AI’s output did not accurately reflect what was given and at times was completely wrong. For example, one image’s output had a person with dirty blonde hair even though I specified him having dark brown hair. This made me curious with what it takes from my input, so I began to test exactly what the AI took by giving simple to complicated prompts until I could no longer generate a photo for some hours.
From my testing, I found out that giving a lot of complicated descriptive prompts about a European man like myself would automatically make a person who was Anglo-looking. I do have some Anglo ancestry, but even when not included in the prompt, this event occurred frequently. It was especially ineffective when given complicated prompts about Slavic or central European features. These outputs make sense as they are based on the data the AI uses, which is predominantly from the Anglo-sphere, therefore, a normal person would be Anglo-looking from its perspective. This should not really infuriate any person too much as the Anglo-sphere is where a lot of AI development is occurring, so the only inevitable fix for this if it really is a problem is for development to move elsewhere.
The AI images above, though looking a little like me, do not fully capture myself as I could not include every physical detail. Both of the images miss the kind of imperfectness of facial features that make a real face. I attempted to fix as much as I could in the remixed version and even distort it through its setting change, but I realized the “making” process was limited to what the AI could process about my human choices.

## Context Statement
- What future does this depict? (Year, key events)
● The future I depict takes place in the year 2043 during winter and displays that a person
has used all of their weekly rate usage of AI and therefore access to AI has been
restricted for 97 hours. On the bottom left, many items are “Decommissioned” implying
complete invalidness or its AI features disabled. To the right of it, there is a section that
allows the person to spend money to increase their AI rate limit. In the “Need Help?”,
section there is reference that this action is from a governmental entity (.gov). This
implies that governmental regulation was taken toward AI usage.
- What present trends led here?
● What inspired me to create my artwork surrounds a real life trend that I experienced
myself. During the last Make 11, when trying to upload my work to my website, I quickly
encountered a message that read “You've hit your session rate limit. Please upgrade your
plan or wait 7 hours 31 minutes for your limit to reset.” This came as a surprise to me
because my AI conversation had not been too long. My experience reveals an aspect
relating to the impacts of AI which is its intensity on resources and infrastructure. The
implied governmental action in my future suggests that as AI usage increased, the more
its impacts affected public life and therefore action was taken.
- What assumptions about human-AI relations does it reveal?
● The most notable assumption my future reveals about human-AI relations is its
integration within society. In the bottom left of the document, the items being
“Decommissioned” are household products. Some of these items like the “water filter”
and “refrigeration” are necessities in the home. While some integration of AI can be seen
as a luxury, the technologies associated with it have been questionably commodified.
This relationship reminds me of the relationship between consumers and car services. An
already installed hardware like heated seats in a car being restricted by a subscription
service to use. The consumer, like the human in my future to AI, is not paying for the
service itself but rather the removal of the lock secured on it by an entity.


## Critical Assignment Reflection
- Is this future desirable? Avoidable? Inevitable?
● The future I made favors an inevitable but possibly avoidable future. What is inevitable is
the strain and intensity AI technologies have on physical & financial infrastructure. I
specifically avoided revealing a specific reason for a weekly rate limit on AI usage. An
imposed limit could be caused by computational costs, a lack of infrastructure,
environmental impacts, or a combination of factors. The reason my future could be
avoidable is that theoretically, if resources are extensively invested in and are of an
adequate quality, there could be enough structure to support the pressure caused by AI.
- What does this future reveal about the present?
● In my future, I depict in the bottom left many items being “Decommissioned” which
either implies it being completely inoperative or only its AI functions being disabled. The
variety of items in the list reveal the present push that corporations (especially tech) do to
integrate AI into every product they manufacture. Why it appears there is less focus from
corporations advertising AI recently like with Microsoft last March removing
unnecessary AI integration in Window 11, there still remains a drive behind its inclusion
in products.
- What would need to change to get to (or avoid) this future?
● What would need to happen for the future depicted in my document to happen is for an
entity whether governmental or corporate to begin locking AI usage in items behind a
calculated limit and/or paywall. The basic outline of this process already exists today
with advanced AI being restricted behind paywalls by companies. To reach the level
depicted in my future, AI would have to become so integrated into society that it bridges
a gap into daily necessities. To be avoided, my future relies on two aspects: current
extensive AI infrastructure investment, and the prevention of financial exploitation of AI
access.

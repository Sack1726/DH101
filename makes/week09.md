## Process Notes
For this make, I used Suno AI in Advanced mode to generate tracks and control both style and lyrics. I selected a high-intensity style direction and then iterated on lyrics through repeated prompt cycles.

The style input emphasized fast, aggressive electronic music with a dark cinematic tone, including descriptors like speedcore, Frenchcore, military extraction atmosphere, metallic textures, and radio-filtered distortion.

I wrote the initial radio-call section myself and then remixed and expanded lyrics with Suno's "Write Full Song" tool. I introduced Frankenstein-inspired language and quotes (for example: "Beware; for I am fearless, and therefore powerful") and repeatedly regenerated outputs until the tracks fit the concept.

In total, I generated 12 tracks from 6 lyric prompts (Suno returns two tracks per generation).

## Reflection
This make made me think about creativity less as "making from scratch" and more as directing, filtering, and deciding. Suno could generate endless variations, but most outputs did not satisfy what I was trying to express. The creative labor came from setting constraints, rewriting prompts, and being willing to discard results that were close but wrong. In that sense, the tool was productive, but not autonomous.

I also noticed that intention was carried most strongly in the parts I authored directly: genre direction, key vocabulary, and lyric edits. Even when the model produced fluent lines, they needed framing and revision to match the concept. The Frankenstein references helped me capture the project's central tension: creating something that feels powerful, strange, and partially outside my control.

This process changed how I define authorship in generative work. I do not see authorship as binary (human or AI), but as layered across prompting, selecting, revising, and sequencing. The final artifact is not just the model's output; it is the record of choices made under abundance. If AI can generate infinitely, then human creativity may lie in taste, limits, and responsibility for what gets published.

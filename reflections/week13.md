# Week 13 Reflection

While it might come as a surprise, I image AI's future as being an in-between heterotopia. In my make, I focused on a present reality about AI usage and features being locked behind a limit or paywall. What I presented differently in the future was the accumulated amount of integration AI had taken place in our lives and as a result became more commodified. While this might make this future sound dystopian, the way social media operates today in our lives might have sounded similar decades ago. This is not to say that they are the same because a unique danger AI integration presents is its ability to spread and taking an over-reliance on it that can cause reduced critical thinking and skills. If this is taken into consideration, our society will be different but not necessarily better or worse off because of trade-offs. Even if AI is integrated to such a degree that its strain forces a limit to be imposed, this does not remove previous or other technologies from being used. In fact, I would say that the future I made that take place in 2043 will still have a significant amount of infrastructure still running on decades old hardware.

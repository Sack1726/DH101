# Reflect 9: What is creativity when AI can generate endlessly? Where is the human in generative work?

I found that the creative aspect with AI generation comes in the form of knowing when to choose and stop. Before continuing, I would like to reference the part of Chapter 7 of Critical Making in the Age of AI that critiques unbiased code. AI has its decisions that reflect biases of its creators and sources. Since AI can generate endlessly, this creates a loop that can only be stopped by the user. When the user stops and selects an output, it is from the specific user's own interests or biases. Through this, the human in generative work is seen with the bias of human choices. My experience in "building the bot" relied on creating lyrics for songs that I felt captured what I was trying to convey (being themes & concepts from Mary Shelley's Frankenstein). This aspect is a human aspect within a grander generative process. The generative process, however, involved aspects not of my choosing. I was in control of the output in its words and genre, but specific beats and lyrical structure were out of my control and belonged to the AI machine. While I had to curate responses that introduced human elements in the generative work, at its core, a lot of the process is carried out by AI. In other words, the output of the work may be similar but not the same and would probably take a much longer time without it.

# Reflect 12: Is AI sustainable? What ecological trade-offs are we willing to accept?

While AI sustainability is theoretically possible, the uncomfortable truth is that a checklist approach rarely works. Since the 1990s, countries have publicly promised environmental action at famous conventions, yet emissions continue to worsen, with many commitments watered down, postponed, or abandoned entirely. True AI sustainability would require a complete societal attitude change. A true answer to what ecological trade-offs of what we as a people are willing to accept will then also change. While you might think I would take a negative likelihood of this occurring, the amount of drastic societal change within the past century proves it may be easier than expected. I fear the modern irrationality in societies today, however, that mixes “what can be measured” with “what is real.” Any ecological trade-off will require measurement and judgment to be made. Treating sustainability and the whole issue as just a quantifiable concern will risk losing the social aspect of sustainability and thus make it impossible for any action to be done. In times like these, I like to confront the question of “Why should I do this?” or “What is motivating me?” Through this, we might see our motivations and reasons toward sustainability. In addition, it might reveal our hypocrisy which should not be avoided because there is plenty of room for it to be acknowledged.

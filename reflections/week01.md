# Reflect 1: What counts as “making” in the age of AI? What might be gained/lost when machines participate in making?

To figure out what counts as "making" in the age of AI, a person must discover what qualities and experiences define them. In other words, what is their voice in this conversation? This can not always be easy to clearly answer, but human experience is what defines personal voices. From this reasoning, what counts as "making" in the age of AI is if the project amplifies or supports a person's own voice. Supporting one's own voice is obviously harder now with AI present, but this is why there is going to be needed interaction within the backdrop of AI in society to explore social and ethical implications. For what would be gained when machines participate in "making" is that a lot of processes that were previously unrealistic or even impossible become much more foreseeable. At the same time, the rise of generative AI that has been designed to generate synthetic content like deepfakes, false news, and hallucinations has sparked the debate regarding the balance between truthful innovation and harmful misinformation in the digital world. This discussion, then, is fundamentally a confrontation of the limits and possibilities of machines in an age of AI where both technological and human processes are constantly evolving.
